Welcome to Dandeli

Heaven in forest

Book Resort

Dandeli is a town in the western Indian state of Karnataka. The Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary, with its trails and dense forests, is home to animals including black panthers, monkeys and elephants, as well as many bird species. A temple stands at the entrance to the limestone Kavala Caves, known for their stalagmite formations. West of the caves, Anshi National Park encompasses the Kali Tiger Reserve

River Rafting

This is our most popular offering where you can experience the fun Rapids, watch shinning scenery & possible wildlife. This was South India’s first of its kind and is one of the most popular attractions.

Zipline

Zip-line is one of the adventure activity at Dandeli. A zip-line consists of a pulley suspended on a cable, usually made of stainless steel, mounted on an incline. Zipline is becoming a popular vacation activity, found at outdoor adventure camps or upscale resorts, where they may be an element on a larger challenge or ropes course.

River Crossing

The river crossing is one of the adventure water sports in Dandeli. River crossings can be dangerous but our certified guide help you to cross the river by using a harness and rope.

Jacuzzi Bath

In Dandeli you get most of the things which are natural. Jacuzzi Bath is one where you get the natural massage from spilling water of river Kali. Nowhere else you find water so playful, so pure, so pacifying, crystal clear waters cascade down the rapids of Kali River gurgling, whispering, laughing & cleansing everything in their way.

Jungle trekking

Jungle trekking is an adventurous journey through the thick jungle where you get to see nature, animals, butterflies & birds. The journey was undertaken on foot in areas where common means of transport is generally not available. Trekking is not mountaineering it is days of walking along with adventure.

Kayaking

Kayaking is the use of a kayak for moving across the water, personnel specialized in kayaking shall guide you through the white water while you start advancing at your own pace. Most kayaks have closed decks, although “sit-on-top” kayaks are growing in popularity, as are inflatable kayaks which come without decks but which have air chambers surrounding the boat.
